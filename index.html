Important Facts about Buddha

  • Siddhartha Gautama, known as the Buddha, was born in Lumbini (modern-day Nepal) around 563 BCE.
  • He was born into a noble family but renounced his wealth and status to seek spiritual enlightenment.
  • Buddha's teachings are centered around the Four Noble Truths: the truth of suffering, the cause of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path to the cessation of suffering.
  • He achieved enlightenment (Nirvana) at Bodh Gaya after meditating under a Bodhi tree for 49 days.
  • Buddha's teachings, known as Buddhism, emphasize the Middle Way—a path between extremes—and the practice of mindfulness and meditation.
  • The Three Jewels of Buddhism are the Buddha (the teacher), the Dharma (the teachings), and the Sangha (the community of practitioners).
  • Buddhism spread across Asia and eventually to other parts of the world, evolving into various traditions and schools.
  • Buddha is often depicted in meditation posture with a serene expression, symbolizing peace and enlightenment.
  • His teachings continue to influence millions of people worldwide, focusing on compassion, ethics, and the pursuit of wisdom.

These points highlight key aspects of Buddha's life, teachings, and impact on global spirituality.
